### Topic 1: U.S.-Iran War & Ceasefire

**Platform Coverage**

**U.S. News Outlets:**

NPR reported on April 6, 2026 that the Iran conflict entered its sixth week, with ongoing missile exchanges. On April 5, NPR reported that Trump issued a profanity-laden social media post warning Iran about the Strait of Hormuz, writing that "Tuesday will be Power Plant Day, and Bridge Day, all wrapped up in one, in Iran," and threatening that if Iran did not open the strait it would be "living in Hell." A U.S. Air Force colonel was shot down over Iran and rescued in a high-risk mission involving dozens of aircraft.
- Source: NPR | "Trump unleashes curse-filled social media rant at Iran after U.S. rescues colonel" | https://www.npr.org/2026/04/05/nx-s1-5774368/iran-war-updates

CNN reported live on April 8–9 that the two-week ceasefire between the U.S. and Iran—brokered by Pakistan—caused oil prices to plummet. West Texas Intermediate crude "tumbled 16.41% to settle at $94.41 per barrel," down from above $110 in the days before the ceasefire. The ceasefire hinged on Iran agreeing to reopen the Strait of Hormuz. Pakistan's position was that Lebanon was included in the ceasefire; the White House and Israel said it was not.
- Source: CNN | "Live updates: US and Iran's fragile ceasefire is being tested" | https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/08/world/live-news/iran-war-trump-us-ceasefire

**Global News Outlets:**

Al Jazeera reported on April 7, 2026 that Trump had warned of the "complete demolition" of key Iranian infrastructure if the Strait of Hormuz was not reopened by his looming deadline. Iran's military dismissed Trump's threats as "delusional."
- Source: Al Jazeera | "Iran war: What is happening on day 39 of US-Israeli attacks?" | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/7/iran-war-what-is-happening-on-day-39-of-us-israeli-attacks

Britannica reported that on April 7 Trump posted on social media: "a whole civilization will die tonight, never to be brought back again." Then on April 7–8 he announced the two-week ceasefire, with Iranian officials confirming that Khamenei's successor had approved the ceasefire after a last-minute nudge by China.
- Source: Britannica | "2026 Iran war" | https://www.britannica.com/event/2026-Iran-war

**X (Twitter):**

A screenshot from X captured by the Institute for the Study of War showed the following trending topics in the United States on or around March 15, 2026: "No Kings," "Ian Happ," and "Taj Bradley." The ISW post on the Iran war had received 4 million views.
- Source: X / Institute for the Study of War post | https://x.com/TheStudyofWar/status/2033260698352976216 [link unconfirmed — screenshot only, not direct page access]

**Social Media / Propaganda Angle:**

CNBC reported on April 1, 2026 that Iran was using Lego-style animated propaganda videos to target American audiences, with four videos posted on the official White House X account on March 5 and 6 garnering "nearly 100 million impressions as of April 1." Iran was described as "blending grievance with meme culture — mixing Epstein, anti-war sentiment and pop visuals to penetrate fragmented Western audiences."
- Source: CNBC | "Iran's war propaganda homes in on Trump with Lego memes" | https://www.cnbc.com/amp/2026/04/01/iran-war-social-media-meme-propaganda.html

Wikipedia's "Media coverage of the 2026 Iran war" article noted that rumors about Netanyahu's death circulated widely on social media, that the White House released videos mixing real airstrike footage with video game and movie clips, and that the FCC chairman warned broadcasters on March 14, 2026 that those who "perpetrate fake news and distort the news" must "take the right course" or risk losing their licenses.
- Source: Wikipedia | "Media coverage of the 2026 Iran war" | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_coverage_of_the_2026_Iran_war

**Public Health Relevance:**
The WHO identified 13 Iranian health infrastructure sites struck during the war. Fuel and pharmaceutical supply chain disruptions have global implications. ICE immigration enforcement changing operations in response to political climate was also cited.

### Topic 2: "No Kings" National Protest Movement

**Platform Coverage**

**U.S. News Outlets:**

NPR reported on March 28, 2026 that protesters took to the streets in cities across the U.S. to oppose Trump policies, energized by "the administration's immigration enforcement tactics and the war in Iran." Organizers planned more than 3,000 events nationwide, with more planned abroad including in Mexico and Canada.
- Source: NPR | "At 'No Kings' rallies, anti-Trump protesters speak out against ICE 'cruelty,' Iran war" | https://www.npr.org/2026/03/28/nx-s1-5763702/no-kings-saturday-protests

The Washington Post reported that "protesters filled the streets Saturday at more than 3,300 rallies across all 50 states for No Kings, a movement that bills itself as nonviolent opposition to what organizers view as authoritarian rulers in the White House and beyond." Demonstrators aired grievances about "the Trump administration trampling democracy, as the president's approval ratings hit new lows."
- Source: The Washington Post | "No Kings protests fill streets at over 3,300 rallies in all 50 states, a record number" | https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2026/03/28/no-kings-protests-marches-record-turnout/

CNN reported that "almost half of protests took place in GOP strongholds," including Texas, Florida, and Ohio (each with over 100 events), and states like Idaho, Wyoming, and Utah with events in the double digits.
- Source: CNN | "March 28, 2026 — 'No Kings' protests take place nationwide" | https://www.cnn.com/2026/03/28/us/live-news/no-kings-protests-03-28-26

**Global News Outlets:**

Al Jazeera covered the protests with photo galleries, noting that parallel events happened internationally in Rome, Paris, and Berlin, and that this was the first major No Kings protest since the U.S.-Israeli war on Iran began one month prior.
- Source: Al Jazeera | "'No Kings' protests erupt across the US, with a Minnesota focus" | https://www.aljazeera.com/gallery/2026/3/28/photos-no-kings-protests-erupt-across-the-us-with-a-minnesota-focus

**X (Twitter):**

"No Kings" appeared as a trending topic in the United States on X, listed alongside "DeNiro" and "Bill Nye" in the captured X sidebar during the protest period (per the ISW post screenshot).
- Source: X sidebar / Institute for the Study of War post screenshot | https://x.com/TheStudyofWar/status/2033260698352976216 [link unconfirmed]

**Movement Website:**

The No Kings official website stated that on March 28, 2026, there were "over 3,100 events; that's more protests than there are counties in the United States." The previous No Kings marches in June and October 2025 drew an estimated 7 million protesters.
- Source: NoKings.org | https://nokingsnyc.com/

Wikipedia's article on the March 2026 No Kings protests stated that the organizers "claim the protests included more than 3,300 organized events across the country that drew a combined estimated eight to nine million protestors. It was the largest single-day protest in American history."
- Source: Wikipedia | "March 2026 No Kings protests" | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/March_2026_No_Kings_protests

**Public Health Relevance:**
The protests highlighted ICE enforcement causing hesitancy to seek medical care (referenced in multiple news sources), and opposition to the Iran war, which has direct humanitarian health implications.

### Topic 3: Artemis II Moon Mission

**Platform Coverage**

**U.S. News Outlets:**

Time magazine reported on April 1, 2026 that NASA's SLS rocket "lifted from its pad" at 6:35 p.m. ET from Kennedy Space Center, with an 8.8 million lb. thrust eruption, carrying a crew of four on a planned 10-day journey around the far side of the moon. The crew includes NASA astronauts Reid Wiseman, Victor Glover, and Christina Koch, along with Canadian Space Agency astronaut Jeremy Hansen.
- Source: Time | "The Lunar Mission the World Is Watching: Inside the Launch of Artemis II" | https://time.com/article/2026/04/01/artemis-ii-moon-launch-what-to-know/

NPR reported on April 6, 2026 that "The Artemis II astronauts have broken a record set by NASA's Apollo 13 astronauts. Once they arrive at the moon late Monday, the crew will loop about 4,000 miles from the lunar surface."
- Source: NPR | U.S. News | https://www.npr.org/sections/national/

**NASA (Primary Source):**

NASA's official Artemis II Flight Day 6 update confirmed that "The Artemis II crew has reached the mission's maximum distance from Earth at 252,756 miles, setting a new record for human spaceflight. This milestone places the crew 4,111 miles farther from Earth than the Apollo 13 mission in 1970."
- Source: NASA | "Artemis II Flight Day 6: Lunar Flyby Updates" | https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/missions/2026/04/06/artemis-ii-flight-day-6-lunar-flyby-updates/

NASA also confirmed: "NASA's SLS (Space Launch System) rocket and Orion spacecraft lifted off from Launch Pad 39B at the agency's Kennedy Space Center in Florida at 6:35 p.m. EDT on April 1, sending the four astronauts on a planned 10-day test flight around the Moon and back."
- Source: NASA | "NASA's Artemis II Mission Leaves Earth Orbit for Flight around Moon" | https://www.nasa.gov/news-release/nasas-artemis-ii-mission-leaves-earth-orbit-for-flight-around-moon/

NASA's blog noted that live coverage aired on "NASA+, Amazon Prime, Apple TV, Hulu, Netflix, HBO Max, and Roku, alongside the agency's 24/7 coverage on its YouTube channel."
- Source: NASA Artemis Blog | https://www.nasa.gov/blogs/artemis/

**CNN (Lunar Flyby):**

CNN's live coverage on April 6 reported that Artemis II Commander Reid Wiseman stated: "On the International Space Station, we're 250 nautical miles up and that's the most beautiful view I think a human could ever experience. And we are dealing with numbers that are 250,000 miles."
- Source: CNN | "Artemis II astronauts break records and take in unprecedented views in historic moon flyby" | https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/06/science/live-news/nasa-artemis-2-flyby-moon-mission

**Public Health Relevance:**
The AVATAR (A Virtual Astronaut Tissue Analog Response) investigation aboard Artemis II uses organ-on-a-chip devices to study the effects of increased radiation and microgravity on human health, with implications for future biomedical research.

### Topic 4: Energy & Gas Price Crisis

**Platform Coverage**

**U.S. News Outlets:**

Al Jazeera reported (March 10, 2026 dateline) that the price of crude oil increased "from about $67 per barrel before the war began on February 28 to nearly $97 on Monday, as the conflict snarls production and transport in one of the most energy-rich regions on earth." The price tracker GasBuddy reported "the average price of gas in the US has risen by 51 cents per gallon over the last week."
- Source: Al Jazeera | "US consumers express dismay over rising gas prices after attack on Iran" | https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/10/us-consumers-express-dismay-over-rising-gas-prices-after-attack-on-iran

CBS News reported that Ian Bremmer, founder of Eurasia Group, expected "average gas prices to reach $4 in the coming week." Trump posted on social media that the rise in oil costs is "a very small price to pay for U.S.A., and World, Safety and Peace."
- Source: CBS News | "Gas prices surge as oil spikes amid Iran war" | https://www.cbsnews.com/news/gas-prices-today-us-iran-war-oil-strait-of-hormuz/

The Washington Times reported on April 8, 2026 that the ceasefire caused oil prices to fall, and that "the national average price per gallon stood at $4.14 on Wednesday." The gas price tracker GasBuddy's Patrick de Haan said on social media that prices "should start easing after 36 hours or so, resulting in a drop of 1 to 3 cents each day by the weekend."
- Source: The Washington Times | "Gas prices could start falling within days, but ceasefire wild cards remain" | https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/apr/8/gas-prices-could-start-falling-within-days-ceasefire-wild-cards/

PBS reported that as of early March 2026, there was a clear "gap between the boom that Trump has predicted and the volatile results he has produced," with rising gasoline prices and job losses threatening to "set the tone in this year's midterm elections."
- Source: PBS NewsHour | "Trump's 'roaring economy' meets a rough start to 2026" | https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/trumps-roaring-economy-meets-a-rough-start-to-2026-with-job-losses-rising-gas-prices-and-uncertainty

**Regional Coverage:**

SAC Media (Sacramento) reported that as of April 2026, gas prices at a Shell station in Carson, California, were displayed above $6.00 per gallon. The report stated that the Iran war "halted oil tanker movement in the Strait of Hormuz, causing a record high surge in prices for gas."
- Source: SAC Media | "California continues to become less affordable as housing costs and gasoline prices surge" | https://sac.media/2026/04/06/california-continues-to-become-less-affordable-as-housing-costs-and-gasoline-prices-surge/

Indiana Governor Mike Braun "declared a gas tax holiday to give Hoosiers relief from the pain at the pump from high gas prices," suspending the state's 7% use tax on fuel (17.2 cents per gallon in April), potentially saving drivers $50 million. In Indiana, a gallon of regular gas cost an average of $4.14 as of the report, up from $3.47 a month ago. Georgia's GOP Gov. Brian Kemp similarly suspended the state's 33.3 cent gas tax in March.
- Source: CNN | "Live updates: US and Iran's fragile ceasefire is being tested" | https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/08/world/live-news/iran-war-trump-us-ceasefire

**Global Energy Context:**

Time magazine reported that the Strait of Hormuz closure had produced "a global oil shortage that is already affecting consumers from Europe to Asia and beyond," with analysts warning that "up to 12 million barrels per day — more than 10% of global demand — potentially affected."
- Source: Time | "The Strait of Hormuz Crisis Is Driving a Wave of Global Energy Rationing" | https://time.com/article/2026/04/05/strait-of-hormuz-fuel-rationing-oil/

**Public Health Relevance:**
Gas price spikes disproportionately burden lower-income households. Oxford Economics' Bernard Yaros noted that the "lower end of the income distribution will cut back even more on discretionary goods and services." Beyond gas, pharmaceutical and medical supply chains from India and Asia are also disrupted. U.S. inflation is projected to rise above 3%.

### Topic 5: Trump Higher Education Reform

**Platform Coverage**

**U.S. News Outlets:**

U.S. News & World Report published a comprehensive overview on March 2, 2026 stating that Trump's "big, beautiful bill" will bring sweeping changes to federal student loans in July 2026, "including setting a total lifetime borrowing limit of $257,500 for all federal student loans." The article noted that federal agencies have targeted "woke" spending, and that the Education Department has withheld "billions of federal dollars from hundreds of institutions."
- Source: U.S. News & World Report | "Trump's Higher Education Crackdown: Visa Revocations, DEI Bans, Lawsuits and Funding Cuts" | https://www.usnews.com/news/national-news/articles/trumps-higher-education-crackdown-visa-revocations-dei-bans-lawsuits-and-funding-cuts

Higher Ed Dive reported on April 4, 2026 that Trump's proposed FY2027 budget "calls for eliminating three key educational access programs and $354 million in grants for minority-serving institutions as part of a plan to shrink the U.S. Department of Education's overall funding." The plan "would lower discretionary funding for the Education Department to $76.5 billion, a 2.9% cut from fiscal 2026. Higher education programs alone would lose $2.7 billion."
- Source: Higher Ed Dive | "How higher ed would fare in Trump's latest budget proposal" | https://www.highereddive.com/news/how-higher-ed-would-fare-in-trumps-latest-budget-proposal/816653/

Inside Higher Ed reported on April 7, 2026 that the Trump administration released draft changes to accreditation standards, wanting accrediting agencies to "refrain from interfering with institutional governance decisions" and to ensure colleges comply with federal laws, "including the prohibition of preferential treatment based on protected characteristics, such as race-based scholarships or programs."
- Source: Inside Higher Ed | "Trump Administration Plans Sweeping Changes to Accreditation" | https://www.insidehighered.com/news/quick-takes/2026/04/07/trump-administration-plans-sweeping-changes-accreditation

EdWeek reported on April 6, 2026 that "the second Trump administration is asking lawmakers to once again consider major cuts" to education, and that "key offices of the Education Department that have seen significant staff reductions in the last year would get substantially less funding." The civil rights office would "lose roughly one-third of its current annual allocation," and the Institute of Education Sciences would see its funding fall "from more than $700 million to just $261 million."
- Source: EdWeek | "Trump Again Proposes Major Education Cuts in New Budget Proposal" | https://www.edweek.org/policy-politics/trump-again-proposes-major-education-cuts-in-new-budget-proposal/2026/04

CNBC reported on February 8, 2026 that Trump's "big beautiful bill" includes new provisions for short-term Pell Grants beginning mid-2026, allowing students enrolling in workforce training programs at community colleges to be eligible for Pell funding worth "up to $7,395 for the 2025-26 academic year." The trend toward shorter credentials—dubbed "un-college"—is accelerating. Employers are projecting "just a 1.6% increase in hiring for the Class of 2026."
- Source: CNBC | "Trump's 'big beautiful bill' may spur significant changes to higher education in 2026" | https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/08/trump-big-beautiful-bill-college-education.html

U.S. News & World Report also noted (January 2, 2026 article) that the number of newly enrolled international students at U.S. colleges dropped by 17% in fall 2025.
- Source: U.S. News & World Report | "The Biggest Developments in Higher Education Policy in 2025" | https://www.usnews.com/news/education-news/articles/2026-01-02/the-biggest-developments-in-higher-education-policy-in-2025

NPR (from the national homepage) listed "Tracking Trump's Higher Ed Reform" as a headline on April 6, 2026.
- Source: NPR | U.S. News | https://www.npr.org/sections/national/

**Public Health Relevance:**
Medical school funding is directly impacted. The U.S. News article noted that "experts say they're concerned about how Trump's signature bill could affect medical students and hospitals." NIH and NSF funding cuts threaten years of public health research infrastructure. The 17% drop in international students affects graduate medical and public health programs.
